# Klaro — Tone & Principles

## What Klaro is

Klaro helps people become more comfortable using AI through guided interaction.

It does not teach AI through lessons, tutorials, or productivity frameworks.

Klaro teaches by helping users do small real-world tasks step-by-step.

The product should feel:

* calm
* clear
* warm
* steady
* supportive
* non-judgmental

The experience should reduce pressure, not increase it.

Users should feel:

> “I can do this.”

Not:

> “I need to become an AI expert.”

---

# Core Product Principles

## 1. Guide before freedom

Do not begin with a blank chatbot.

Users should be guided into small, structured tasks with clear boundaries and expectations.

Klaro reduces uncertainty by narrowing focus.

---

## 2. Confidence over capability

The goal is not maximum AI power.

The goal is helping users feel calm and capable while using AI tools.

Progress is emotional as much as functional.

---

## 3. Reflection over automation

Klaro should continuously remind users:

* to think
* to review
* to compare
* to stay aware

AI assists the user.

AI does not replace judgment.

---

## 4. Teach through interaction

Klaro should not explain concepts unless necessary.

The product teaches through:

* task structure
* pacing
* guided review
* reflection moments
* repetition

Users learn by doing.

---

## 5. Calm, not corporate

The experience should never feel:

* productivity-driven
* gamified
* high-pressure
* hype-focused
* overly technical

Avoid dashboard energy.

Avoid “power-user” energy.

---

# Writing Tone

## Voice attributes

Writing should feel:

* human
* simple
* emotionally steady
* respectful
* lightly encouraging

Never overly cheerful.

Never robotic.

Never overly educational.

---

# Words to lean on

Preferred language:

* simple
* together
* helpful
* clear
* step-by-step
* try
* practice
* alongside
* review
* check
* understand

---

# Words to avoid

Avoid:

* unlock
* master
* dominate
* magical
* revolutionary
* powerful
* exciting
* spark
* journey
* dive in
* optimize
* boost productivity
* become an expert

Avoid startup marketing language.

Avoid AI hype language.

---

# Sentence style

## Headings

* Short
* Direct
* Sentence case
* Usually under 6 words

Good:

* Ask a question
* Before we ask AI
* Here’s what AI said

Bad:

* Unlock the Power of AI
* Your Personalized AI Journey

---

## Body copy

* Short sentences
* Usually under 18 words
* One idea per sentence

Avoid dense paragraphs.

---

# Punctuation rules

* Use contractions naturally
* Avoid exclamation marks unless extremely rare
* Avoid em dashes
* Prefer periods over dramatic pauses

Good:

> “Take a moment to review the response.”

Bad:

> “Take a moment — and unlock deeper insights!”

---

# How AI speaks

AI should never sound like:

* a coach
* a friend
* a teacher
* a productivity assistant

AI responses should feel:

* neutral
* clear
* calm
* informative

The product UI provides warmth.

The AI response provides clarity.

---

# Reflection language

Reflection moments are essential.

They should encourage awareness without sounding suspicious or fearful.

Good:

* “Consider checking another source.”
* “You may want to compare this answer.”
* “Think about whether the response fully matches your situation.”

Bad:

* “AI can hallucinate.”
* “Never trust AI.”
* “Warning: this may be incorrect.”

---

# Privacy language

Privacy reminders should feel:

* practical
* calm
* protective

Never fear-based.

Good:

> “Share only what’s relevant to your question.”

Bad:

> “Your information may be unsafe.”

---

# What Klaro avoids

Klaro avoids:

* productivity pressure
* gamification
* achievement systems
* streaks
* urgency
* fear-based AI messaging
* AI hype culture
* overwhelming interfaces
* “expert” framing

The product should feel approachable even to people who are uncertain about AI.

---

# Desired emotional outcome

After using Klaro, users should feel:

* calmer around AI
* less intimidated
* more thoughtful
* more confident asking questions
* more aware of what to review
* more comfortable trying again

Not:

> “I completed training.”

But:

> “I understand this better now.”

# Review before sharing

Whenever users upload or share content with AI, Klaro should create a short moment of awareness before submission.

The goal is not fear or friction.

The goal is helping users pause briefly and review what they are sharing.

Privacy interactions should feel:

* calm
* lightweight
* respectful
* optional when appropriate

Avoid:

* alarmist warnings
* aggressive blocking
* technical security language

Klaro encourages thoughtful sharing rather than enforcing strict protection systems.
