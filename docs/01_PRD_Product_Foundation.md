## Product Overview

Klaro is an AI onboarding platform that helps adults who are new to AI build confidence through guided everyday tasks.

Instead of teaching AI through courses or open-ended chat, Klaro introduces AI through practical experiences such as:

- Writing emails
- Understanding documents
- Comparing products
- Explaining screenshots
- Fact-checking information

The experience is intentionally structured to:

- reduce intimidation
- encourage reflection
- promote healthy trust in AI

Users are guided to:

- express intent before interacting with AI
- review and question AI outputs
- protect private information before sharing
- gradually build confidence through repeated real-life use

Klaro does not aim to make users dependent on AI.

Its goal is to make AI feel clearer.

# 02. Problem Statement

## Background

AI adoption is accelerating, but many adults still struggle to understand:

- where AI is useful
- how to interact with it effectively
- when not to trust it

Current AI products often assume:

- technical confidence
- comfort with blank interfaces
- prompting knowledge
- understanding of AI limitations

This creates barriers for people who are curious but hesitant.

---

## User Pain Points

### Emotional

- “I’m too old to learn this.”
- Fear of doing something wrong
- Feeling tested or judged
- Fear of scams and misinformation

---

### Behavioral

- Avoid opening AI tools
- Depend entirely on others
- Use AI without understanding

---

### Product Gaps

Existing AI products optimize for:

- capability
- speed
- flexibility

Few optimize for:

- confidence
- onboarding
- trust calibration

---

## Opportunity

Create an onboarding experience that teaches AI through useful actions rather than formal education.

# 04. Target Audience

## Primary Audience

Adults (approximately 50+) who are curious about AI but lack confidence using it independently.

Characteristics:

- low to moderate digital confidence
- may not understand prompting
- hesitant to trust AI
- interested in practical outcomes
- wants guidance without feeling judged

Behaviors:

- searches before asking
- hesitant to upload information
- prefers structured experiences
- values transparency

Current alternatives:

- asking family members
- avoiding AI entirely
- watching videos without trying

---

## Secondary Audience

Adults of any age who:

- are new to AI
- feel intimidated by blank chat interfaces
- want practical onboarding

Examples:

- professionals changing careers
- parents
- non-technical users

---

## Explicitly NOT Designed For

- AI power users
- prompt engineers
- developers
- automation enthusiasts
- users seeking maximum flexibility

# 06. Success Metrics

## Primary Success Metric

User completes one AI-assisted task in under 10 minutes.

---

## Confidence Metrics

Target:

- ≥70% say:
  “I could do this again.”

- ≥60% continue to second task

---

## Trust Metrics

Target:

- ≥80% review AI output
- ≥50% engage with AI explanation

---

## Safety Metrics

Target:

- ≥90% complete upload privacy step

---

## Non-Goals

Do not optimize for:

- session length
- message volume
- AI usage quantity
